BIPOC Healing & Wellness Centre

Noble Building, 8540 109 Street NW, #401

Edmonton, AB

T6G 1E6

1 587 760-1311

admin@bipochealingcentre.ca

https://www.bipochealingcentre.ca

All of the BIPCO Healing & Wellness Centre team members are racialized people, and serve community members who identify as Black, Indigenous or People of Colour. They specialize in therapy for BIPOC mental health, BIPOC grief, intergenerational trauma, racial trauma, healing from experiences of racism and discrimination, racial identity issues and identity-based trauma, cultural identity, as well as newcomer, immigrant and refugee mental health. Grief therapy is through an intersectional lens that provides culturally sensitive support.

Horizon Health Network Spiritual Care

NB

1 888 820-5444

https://horizonnb.ca/services/support-and-therapy/therapeutic-services/spiritual-care

Service offered in hospitals across New Brunswick, and one of the requests that can be made for spiritual care is for grief. The practitioners are trained members of the health care teams at hospitals, and religious care is available upon request. Indigenous cultural care is also available. Offered in Moncton/Sackville, Saint John, Fredericton, Miramichi, and Upper River Valley.

Hospice Northwest's volunteers provide one-to-one palliative support to individuals and families in the home, hospitals, hospices, and long-term care facilities. Hospice of Waterloo Region operates the Gies Family Centre, a palliative care centre that includes the 10-bed Cook Family Residence. A range of support services for those living in the community is provided by professional counsellors and volunteers. One-to-one and group bereavement support is available, including specialized groups for Indigenous and Rainbow communities.

St. Joseph's Care Group - Hospice/Palliative Care

Thunder Bay, ON

St. Joseph's Hospital has 10 hospice beds and 11 palliative care beds for adults. One-to-one grief support is offered to in-patient clients, adults, children, and families on the hospice palliative care unit. Clients also have access to Indigenous Services that include an Indigenous Counsellor, Indigenous Health Transition Coordinator, and Cultural practitioners with the Traditional Healing Program at St. Joseph's Care Group, which hosts the North West Regional Palliative Care Program

Hill House Hospice

36 Wright Street

Richmond Hill, ON

L4C 4A1

1 905 737-9308

info@hillhousehospice.com

http://www.hillhousehospice.com/

Hill House Hospice is a residential hospice that provides compassionate and expert medical care at no cost to people living with a life-limiting illness. It offers a home-like environment with private bedrooms, nutritious meals, respecting religious or dietary preferences, 24/7 medical care, high staff to resident ratio including physicians, nurses, and personal support workers, music therapy; complementary therapy including light massage, aromatherapy, reiki, and therapeutic touch. Grief and bereavement support is provided to families and caregivers.
